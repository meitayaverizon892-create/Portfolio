Security Awareness

Cybersecurity Awareness Guide

A practical guide covering the most common cyber threats and exactly how individuals and businesses can protect themselves online.

AudienceGeneral / SMEs
FormatInteractive Guide
Topics6 Threat Areas
StatusComplete
🎣
Phishing Attacks

Fake emails that trick you into revealing passwords or clicking malicious links.

Protection: Verify sender addresses. Never click suspicious links. Use email filters.
HIGH RISK
🦠
Malware & Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts your files and demands payment to restore access.

Protection: Keep software updated. Use antivirus. Back up files regularly.
HIGH RISK
🔑
Weak Passwords

Passwords that are too short, reused, or predictable — easily cracked by brute force.

Protection: Use 12+ character passwords. Enable 2FA. Use a password manager.
MEDIUM RISK
📡
Unsecured Wi-Fi

Public or poorly configured networks that allow attackers to intercept your traffic.

Protection: Use VPN on public Wi-Fi. Avoid banking on public networks.
MEDIUM RISK
👤
Social Engineering

Manipulating people into revealing confidential information through deception.

Protection: Verify identities before sharing info. Train staff on awareness.
HIGH RISK
💾
Data Breaches

Unauthorised access to databases exposing personal and financial information.

Protection: Encrypt stored data. Use access controls. Monitor system logs.
